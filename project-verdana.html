Overview

Verdana House is a new single-family residence nestled within the forested landscape of New York's Hudson Valley. The project responds to its site with a formal restraint that allows the architecture to recede — and the landscape to speak.


A long, low profile of weathered steel and white oak anchors the home to the ground plane, while a series of large-format glazed openings dissolve the boundary between interior and exterior. The result is a sequence of rooms that feel both sheltered and exposed — intimate, yet connected to the sky above.


The interior program flows around a central hearth, organizing living, dining, and sleeping into a loose, open-plan arrangement that allows daylight to travel deeply into the plan throughout the day.
